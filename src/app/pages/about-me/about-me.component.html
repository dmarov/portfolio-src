My principles

At present I realize what I've understood a lot from my experience and would like to formulate it in a list of principles:

  • I consider that development should start with interface rather than implementation, because the main challenge of development is to control complexity, and thought through interface manages to do so.
  • I consider that it's worth following SOLID principles, because it helps to write reliable and reusable code. The most important ones I consider "Single Responsibility" and "Open-Closed" principles.
  • I consider that the usefulness of inheritance is often overrated. It is appropriate in some cases, but most of the time, object composition is preferable, because inheritance creates the tightest coupling
  • I consider that refactoring should be regarded as a part of a task rather than a separate process
  • I consider the ability to ask correct questions is the most valuable skill a developer can have
  • I am a strong advocate for uniform code formatting, because it reduces distractions and helps us focus on what's essential, which is software architecture
  • I am a strong advocate for fast code integration methods like trunk-based development, as it significantly reduces bugs caused by intricate conflicts
  • I consider it's important to progress in small steps and obtain fast feedback at the end of each step in order to avoid wasting time and money on features, which are not needed
  • I consider the importance of appropriate naming to be significantly underrated in general. It is the hardest part in software development and yet receives insufficient attention
  • I consider that every decision in software development is a trade-off
